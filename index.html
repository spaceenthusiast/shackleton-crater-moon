Shackleton Crater is a 21 km-wide impact crater at the Moon's South Pole. Its rim receives near-constant sunlight, while the interior remains in permanent shadow — one of the coldest places in the solar system. Scientists believe it may harbor water ice deposits trapped in its shadowed floor, making it a prime target for future lunar exploration, resource utilization, and Artemis mission landing sites.
